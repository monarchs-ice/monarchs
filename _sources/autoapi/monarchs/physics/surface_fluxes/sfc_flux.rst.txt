monarchs.physics.surface_fluxes.sfc_flux
========================================

.. py:function:: monarchs.physics.surface_fluxes.sfc_flux(alpha, lid, lake, lw_in, sw_in, air_temp, p_air, dew_point_temperature, wind, xsurf)

   Calculate the surface heat flux from the input shortwave and longwave
   fluxes and latent/sensible heat fluxes.

   :Parameters: * **alpha** (*float*) -- Surface albedo.
                * **lid** (*bool*) -- Flag which indicates whether there is a lid at the surface due to
                  refreezing. Used here to determine which form of bulk fluxes to use.
                * **lake** (*bool*) -- Flag which indicates whether there is a lake present. Used here to
                  determine which form of bulk fluxes to use.
                * **lw_in** (*float*) -- Incoming longwave radiation. [W m^-2].
                * **sw_in** (*float*) -- Incoming shortwave (solar) radiation. [W m^-2].
                * **air_temp** (*float*) -- Surface-layer air temperature. [K].
                * **p_air** (*float*) -- Surface-layer air pressure. [hPa].
                * **dew_point_temperature** (*float*) -- Dew-point temperature at the surface. [K]
                * **wind** (*float*) -- Wind speed. [m s^-1].
                * **xsurf** (*float*) -- Surface temperature. Taken from our initial guess x (i.e. x[0]) [K].

   :returns: **Q** -- Surface energy flux. [W m^-2].
   :rtype: float64
